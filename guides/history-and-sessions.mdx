CodeAura saves your work automatically as you go. Every significant change creates or updates a history entry, giving you a timestamped record of your code images without any manual save step. Sign in to push that history to the cloud and access it from any device.

## How automatic saving works

CodeAura uses a **smart session** strategy to decide whether a change should create a new history entry or silently update the current one.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="New entry created">
    A brand-new history entry is created when you make a **content or language change**:

    - Pasting or typing new code
    - Switching the programming language

    These are considered distinct creative sessions and are saved as separate entries with a new timestamp.
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="Current entry updated">
    The most recent entry is **updated in place** — without creating a duplicate — when you adjust **style settings** only:

    - Changing the theme, background, or font
    - Adjusting padding, shadow, or window style
    - Toggling line numbers or indent guides

    This prevents minor visual tweaks from flooding your history with near-identical entries.
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

<Note>
If the most recent history entry is **pinned**, CodeAura will never mutate it. Instead, any change that would normally update it creates a new entry instead, keeping your pinned work intact.
</Note>

## History storage

History is stored in your browser's **IndexedDB** database. This means:

- History is available immediately, even with no internet connection.
- History is scoped to the browser and device where you created it.
- Up to **50 entries** are retained. When the limit is reached, the oldest unpinned entry is removed automatically to make room for the new one. Pinned entries are never evicted.

## Viewing the history panel

Click the **History** icon in the toolbar to open the history panel. Each entry shows:

- A preview of the code snippet
- The programming language and theme used
- A timestamp
- A pin icon to mark it as important

Click any entry to restore it to the editor, including all style settings at the time it was saved.

## Pinning entries

Pin an entry to protect it from automatic eviction and mark it for easy retrieval.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the history panel">
    Click the **History** icon in the top toolbar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the entry to pin">
    Scroll through the list to find the entry you want to keep permanently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Click the pin icon">
    Click the pin icon on the entry card. A filled pin indicates the entry is protected. Click it again to unpin.
  </Step>
</Steps>

Pinned entries:

- Are never removed when the 50-entry limit is reached.
- Are not deleted when you use **Clear history**.
- Are synced to the server with priority when you sign in.

## Clearing history

Click **Clear history** in the history panel to remove all unpinned entries. Pinned entries are left untouched. This action removes entries from IndexedDB immediately and cannot be undone for unsynced sessions.

## Cross-device sync

By default, history lives only in the browser where it was created. Sign in to your CodeAura account to sync history to the server and access it from any device.

### How sync works

<Steps>
  <Step title="Sign in">
    Click **Sign in** in the top-right corner and authenticate with your account. CodeAura uses your access token to identify unsynced entries.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Initial sync">
    On first sign-in, CodeAura automatically pushes all local entries that have never been synced to the server. Each entry is identified by a unique `syncId` (a UUID) so duplicates are never created if you sign in from multiple browsers.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ongoing sync">
    After the initial push, new entries and updates are synced to the server in the background. Entries show a sync indicator once they have been confirmed by the server.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Access from another device">
    Sign in on a second device and your synced history is pulled down automatically. Any local-only entries on that device are merged with your cloud history.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
Sync requires an active internet connection. If a sync attempt fails, CodeAura retries silently in the background — no entries are lost. Local IndexedDB always acts as the source of truth when offline.
</Note>

### Sync status indicators

Each history entry carries a sync status:

| Status | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Unsynced | Saved locally only. Will be pushed on next sync. |
| Synced | Confirmed by the server and available on all signed-in devices. |
