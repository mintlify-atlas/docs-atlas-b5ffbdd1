title: "Get started with CodeAura"
description: "Learn how to create your first code snapshot with CodeAura in four steps: paste code, choose a theme, style the frame, then export or share."
---

This guide walks you through creating your first code snapshot with CodeAura. You will go from a blank editor to a polished, exportable image in under two minutes — no account or installation required.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open CodeAura">
    Navigate to the CodeAura web app in your browser. The editor opens immediately with a sample TypeScript snippet pre-loaded so you can see what a finished snapshot looks like before you add your own code.

    <Tip>
      CodeAura saves your work to local history automatically. You can close the tab and pick up exactly where you left off when you return.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add your code">
    Click inside the editor and replace the sample code with your own snippet, or paste it directly with **Ctrl+V** / **Cmd+V**.

    CodeAura detects your programming language automatically and applies syntax highlighting. If the detection is wrong, use the language dropdown at the top of the editor to select the correct language from the full list of 50+ options.

    You can also set a file name (for example `main.go`) in the file name field above the editor. This name appears in the window title bar of your snapshot.

    <Note>
      Languages with full CodeMirror support — including JavaScript, TypeScript, Python, Rust, Java, C++, HTML, CSS, JSON, SQL, PHP, and more — receive active syntax highlighting. All other languages are recognized for labeling but render in plain text mode.
    </Note>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Customize the appearance">
    Use the panels on the right side of the editor to style your snapshot:

    - **Theme** — Pick from 25 themes across four categories: dark (Dracula, One Dark Pro, Tokyo Night, Catppuccin Mocha, Nord, and more), light (GitHub Light, Solarized Light, One Light, and more), high contrast (GitHub Dark HC, Pitch Black, Cobalt2), and retro (Amber Terminal, Vaporwave, Hacker (Matrix)).
    - **Background** — Choose a gradient, a solid color, or a transparent background.
    - **Frame** — Adjust the padding around your code, toggle the macOS-style window controls, and set the drop shadow depth.
    - **Font** — Select a developer-friendly font and tune the font size, line height, and weight.

    The preview updates live as you make changes.

    <Tip>
      Significant changes — like switching the language or replacing the code — create a new history entry. Style tweaks like adjusting padding or switching themes update your current entry in place, keeping your history clean.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Export or share">
    When your snapshot looks the way you want, open the **Export** menu and choose how to save or share it:

    - **Download** — Save the image to your computer as PNG, JPEG, SVG, or WebP. The default scale is 2× for sharp, high-resolution output.
    - **Copy to clipboard** — Copy the image directly so you can paste it into a chat, email, or slide deck.
    - **Shareable link** — Generate a unique URL so anyone can open and remix your snapshot. Sign in to make the link persistent across devices.

    <Note>
      Shareable links require a free CodeAura account. Downloaded images are available without signing in.
    </Note>
  </Step>
</Steps>
